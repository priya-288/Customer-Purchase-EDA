##  Customer Purchase Behavior - Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)

> An end-to-end Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) project that uncovers customer purchasing behavior, identifies high-value customer segments, and provides actionable business recommendations to improve marketing performance and revenue.

---

### Project Overview

Understanding customer behavior is essential for designing effective marketing campaigns and improving customer retention. This project analyzes customer demographics, purchasing patterns, campaign responses, and product preferences to identify business opportunities using Python.

---

### Business Problem

The aims is to improve customer engagement and campaign effectiveness by understanding how demographic factors and purchasing behavior influence customer loyalty and product adoption.

Objectives:

* Identify high-value customer segments
* Analyze purchasing behavior across product categories
* Understand customer preferences by demographics
* Analyze Customer Campaign Response Patterns
* Generate actionable business recommendations

---

### Dataset Information

* Total Customers: 2,240
* Features: 29
* Categories Covered:
  * Customer Demographics
  * Income
  * Family Composition
  * Product Spending
  * Purchase Channels
  * Marketing Campaigns
  * Customer Complaints

## Key Business Insights

* 49.2% of customers belong to the 25–45 age group, making it the company's largest customer segment.
* 64.5% of customers are Married, while 50.3% hold a Graduation degree.
* Spain accounts for 48.8% of the customer base, making it the primary market.
* Customers aged 25–45 generate 44–47% of total revenue across all product categories.
* Wine is the highest revenue-generating product, followed by Meat Products.
* Customers aged 18–25 have the highest average spending per customer despite representing only 2.7% of customers.
* Store Purchases (12,970) significantly exceed Web (9150) and Catalog Purchases (5,963), indicating a strong preference for in-store shopping.
* Customers in the Very High Income segment have the highest campaign response rate (~27%).
* PhD holders (~21%) and Widowed customers (~25%) show the strongest campaign engagement.
* Campaign acceptance remains relatively low overall, highlighting the need for better customer segmentation and personalized marketing.

---

# Business Recommendations

* Prioritize marketing campaigns for customers aged 25–45 and 45–60, who contribute nearly 80% of total revenue.
* "Promote Wine and Meat Products through personalized promotions and targeted product recommendations."
* Develop premium loyalty programs for High and Very High Income customers.
* Improve campaign targeting for Married customers, who represent 64.5% of the customer base but have the lowest response rate.
* Expand personalized digital marketing while maintaining a strong in-store customer experience.

---


## Conclusion

The analysis reveals that the 25–45 and 45–60 age groups are the company's most valuable customer segments, contributing nearly 80% of total product revenue. Wine and Meat Products are the primary revenue drivers, while Store Purchases remain the preferred shopping channel. Income strongly influences customer spending, and campaign response varies significantly across customer segments. These insights provide a strong foundation for personalized marketing strategies, improved customer retention, and sustainable revenue growth.
